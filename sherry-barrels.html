Flavor Transformation

Sherry Types & Applications

Each sherry type imparts distinct organoleptic properties, allowing you to craft precise flavor profiles for your spirits and beers.

Fino

Biological Aging | Crisp & Mineral

Flavor Profile

Delicate almond and chamomile notes with bright acetaldehydic compounds. Preserves base spirit character while adding crisp minerality and perceived dryness.

Best Applications

  • Spirits: Light whiskies, premium vodka, gin finishing
  • Beer: Saisons, farmhouse ales, light lagers

Manzanilla

Coastal Aging | Saline & Delicate

Flavor Profile

Distinctive saline and sea-breeze character with green almond and subtle herbal notes. The coastal influence adds unique brine complexity and enhanced minerality.

Best Applications

  • Spirits: Coastal gin, light rum, delicate whiskies
  • Beer: Berliner Weisse, gose, coastal-inspired ales

Amontillado

Dual Aging | Balanced Complexity

Flavor Profile

Hazelnut, toasted almond, and herbal undertones with mild caramel. Perfect balance of biological freshness and oxidative depth.

Best Applications

  • Spirits: Single malt whisky, aged rum, a√±ejo tequila
  • Beer: Belgian strong ales, brown ales, mixed fermentation

Oloroso

Oxidative Aging | Rich & Robust

Flavor Profile

Intense walnut, dried fig, dark chocolate, and leather notes. High polyphenol content creates velvety mouthfeel and long, warming finishes.

Best Applications

  • Spirits: Single malt whisky, dark rum, aged brandies
  • Beer: Imperial stouts, barleywines, strong dark ales

Palo Cortado

Rare Hybrid | Elegant Complexity

Flavor Profile

Delicate citrus peel, subtle balsamic undertones, and toffee layers. Combines Amontillado finesse with Oloroso power in rare harmony.

Best Applications

  • Spirits: Premium whisky, luxury rum, craft spirits
  • Beer: Wild ales, limited editions, complex blends

Sweet Intensity | Dessert Character

Flavor Profile

Powerful raisin, fig, molasses, and dark honey notes. High residual sugars and caramelized compounds create lush, dessert-like profiles.

Best Applications

  • Spirits: Sweet whiskies, premium rum, dessert brandies
  • Beer: Pastry stouts, sweet barleywines, experimental ales
